Questions

Good to know

What does Sabr do?

Sabr blocks the apps that distract you and lets you earn screen time back by completing a short Islamic learning challenge — a quick quiz or memory match. It also tracks your five daily prayers, and Prayer Guard pauses your distracting apps at prayer time until you've prayed. It launches with the 99 Names of Allah, and more challenges (Daily Duas, short Surahs, Seerah) are on the way.

How does Prayer Guard work?

Sabr calculates your five daily prayer times for your location and reminds you before each salah. When a prayer comes in, Prayer Guard pauses your distracting apps for that window and keeps them paused until you mark the prayer as prayed — a gentle nudge to step away and pray. Every day you keep all five grows your prayer streak. Prayer times are computed entirely on your device.

How much does it cost?

The core learn-and-earn features are free. An optional Premium subscription unlocks unlimited app limits and quiet-hours sessions. Pricing is shown in the app before any purchase.

Does Sabr collect any of my data?

No. There is no account, no analytics, and no advertising. Your rules, progress, and usage stay on your device. Read the full Privacy Policy.

Why does it need Screen Time permission?

Sabr uses Apple's Family Controls and Device Activity frameworks to block the apps you choose. This is processed entirely by Apple's system on your device — Sabr never reads the underlying data, and you can revoke it anytime in Settings → Screen Time.

What do I need to run it?

An iPhone running iOS 17 or later. Sabr will be available on the App Store soon.
