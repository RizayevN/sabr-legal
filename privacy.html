Privacy Policy — Sabr

Effective date: 18 June 2026

App: Sabr (iOS)

Developer: Nematzhon Rizayev

Summary

Sabr is designed to work entirely on your device. **We do not collect, store, or

transmit your personal data.** Sabr has no account system, no analytics, and no

advertising. It does not have its own servers.

What Sabr accesses, and why

Screen Time data (Family Controls / Device Activity).

With your permission, Sabr uses Apple's Family Controls and Device Activity

frameworks to block the apps and websites you choose and to show your usage

summary. This information is processed only on your device by Apple's

system. Sabr cannot read the underlying data, and none of it is ever sent off

your device or shared with us or any third party. You can revoke this permission

at any time in Settings → Screen Time.

Your blocking rules and learning progress.

The apps you choose to limit, your schedules, your quiz/match results, and your

progress through the 99 Names are stored locally on your device (in an app

group container and a local file). They are not uploaded anywhere. Deleting the

app removes this data from your device.

Notifications.

If you allow notifications, Sabr sends you local reminders (for example, that

you can earn screen time, or that an earned window has ended). These are

generated on your device. Sabr does not use push notifications and does not

collect a device token.

Purchases

Subscriptions (Sabr Premium) are processed by Apple through the App Store. Sabr

uses Apple's StoreKit to verify your subscription status on your device. We do

not receive your name, payment details, or Apple Account information. Apple's

handling of your purchase is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.

Data we collect

None. Sabr does not collect any data linked to your identity, does not track

you across apps or websites, and contains no third-party SDKs.

Children

Sabr does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including

children. The app is rated 4+.

Your rights (GDPR / CCPA)

Because Sabr stores everything locally and collects no personal data, there is no

personal information for us to access, correct, export, or delete on a server.

You remain in full control of the on-device data and can erase it by deleting

the app.

Changes

If this policy changes, we will update the effective date above and post the

revised policy at the same URL.
